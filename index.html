Student Organizations

Pig Flying Society (PFS)

This organization offers pig-flying lessons. Got a pig that can't fly? Then PFS is the place for you. Our expert instructors provide comprehensive training sessions designed to teach your pig the art of flying. We offer on-campus assistance and discounted prices for all students, ensuring that our services are accessible and affordable. At PFS, we believe in making the impossible possible. Our curriculum includes safety protocols, flight techniques, and confidence-building exercises for both pigs and their owners. Join us for regular workshops, special events, and community gatherings where you can share your progress and experiences with fellow enthusiasts. Whether you're a beginner or looking to refine your pig's flying skills, PFS is dedicated to helping you achieve your flying dreams.

Single Kids Club

This is an organization that provides a safe space for students not in relationships to gather and fellowship. This club offers a supportive community where members can share their experiences, find solidarity, and build meaningful friendships. We host regular meetings, social events, and workshops that focus on personal development, mental wellness, and self-love. Our goal is to create a safety net where members can express all the anger and oppression they feel from people in relationships, while also celebrating the joys and benefits of being single. Through open discussions and activities, we strive to foster a sense of belonging and empowerment among our members, reminding them that being single is a valid and fulfilling way of life.

Honors Society

This organization is strictly for students who have a 3.8 GPA and above. Legally, we have to treat them better, so we created an organization just for them. The Honors Society is dedicated to recognizing and rewarding academic excellence. Members enjoy exclusive benefits, including access to advanced academic resources, personalized mentorship programs, and opportunities for prestigious internships. We host regular meetings where members can network with faculty, industry professionals, and fellow high achievers. Our events calendar is packed with academic workshops, leadership seminars, and cultural outings, all designed to foster both intellectual and personal growth. In addition, the Honors Society provides a platform for members to engage in meaningful community service projects, enhancing their resumes and making a positive impact. Membership in the Honors Society is not only a mark of distinction but also a gateway to unparalleled opportunities and lifelong connections.

Campus News

University receives $12,000,000 donation

The university is thrilled to announce a generous $12,000,000 donation from an anonymous benefactor. This unprecedented gift will significantly enhance the institution’s ability to provide high-quality education and resources to its students. The funds are earmarked for several key initiatives, including the expansion of scholarship programs, the enhancement of research facilities, and the development of new academic programs. Additionally, a portion of the donation will be allocated to improving campus infrastructure, ensuring that the university remains a state-of-the-art environment for learning and innovation. The administration extends its deepest gratitude to the donor for their remarkable contribution, which will have a lasting impact on the university community. A formal ceremony to celebrate this monumental donation will be held next month, with details to be announced soon.

SGA Week

The Student Government Association (SGA) is excited to announce SGA Week, a week-long celebration dedicated to fostering community spirit and student engagement. This annual event will feature a variety of activities designed to entertain, educate, and empower students. Highlights of the week include the SGA Fair, where students can learn about different clubs and organizations on campus, and the Leadership Workshop Series, offering valuable skills for personal and professional development. Other events include charity fundraisers, cultural showcases, and a campus-wide talent show. Throughout the week, students will have the opportunity to voice their opinions and provide feedback on university policies during open forums and town hall meetings. SGA Week is not only a chance to have fun and make new friends but also a platform to influence positive change within the university. Be sure to check the full schedule of events and join in the festivities!

Final call to pick up computers!

This is the final call for students to pick up their allocated computers from the university’s IT department. All students who applied for and were approved to receive a computer must collect their devices by the end of this week. The distribution center, located in the Main Library, will be open from 9 AM to 5 PM, Monday through Friday. Please bring a valid student ID and a copy of the approval email to ensure a smooth pickup process. Unclaimed computers will be reassigned to other students in need after the deadline. If you have any questions or need to arrange an alternate pickup time, please contact the IT support team at itsupport@university.edu. Don’t miss this final opportunity to collect your device and ensure you have the necessary tools for your academic success.
